Lafayette House

Lafayette House
600 E. Simpson Street

Built 1892
5BL.823
National Register 05/20/1983;
Local Register 09/21/1999
 

The Lafayette House was constructed in 1892 and opened for business as a boarding house at the turn of the century.  Rooms cost fifty cents a night or three dollars a week, including meals!  Boarding houses played an integral role in Lafayette's development.  Many of the men who worked in the mines were single or lived alone until they could afford to send for their families.  The Lafayette House was home for the Baldwin-Fells detectives who were brought to town by the mine owners to break the strike of 1910.

Miller House

Miller House
409 E. Cleveland Street

Built 1900
5BL.818
National Register 05/20/1983;
Local Register 09/21/1999
 

Lafayette is in many ways the town Mary Miller built, and this was Mary Miller's home in Lafayette. The sandstone walks and bay windows on the east and west sides of the building, as well as the iron fence that surrounds the property, are all as they were when the home was built in 1900.
Mary and Lafayette Miller arrived in the area from Iowa in 1863. They are credited with bringing the area's first threshing machine. In 1871 the Millers bought the land that would later become the Lafayette townsite, and opened a hotel and stage station on the Denver to Laramie stage route. In 1878 Lafayette Miller died suddenly, leaving Mary a widow with six children. She continued to run their farm with the help of her sons. In 1884 coal was discovered on the Miller's property, and Mrs. Miller leased the Simpson Mine on the southeast corner of her ranch, and developed city of Lafayette on the rest of her acreage.

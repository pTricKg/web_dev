Lafayette High School

Lafayette High School
101 E. Baseline Road

Built1925
5BL.10401
Local Register 12/09/2008
 

Two portions of the former Lafayette High School building have been landmarked – the original portion built in 1925 (representative of the Classical Revival style applied to high school buildings in Colorado in the 1920-30s), and the western-most gymnasium built in 1952 with is barrel roof. Prominent Colorado architect Robert K. Fuller designed the original school.
The original 1925 building still retains the same roof form, exterior materials, window and entrance embellishments and dentiled cornice, but its integrity has been impacted by seven additions. It is the oldest school in the city and the building’s evolution from high school to middle school to grade school reflects the community’s growth and expansion during the 20th century and into the 21st century.

Swennes House

Swennes House
410 W. Cleveland Street

Built 1944
5BL.8889
Local Register 06/19/2007
 

Built in 1944, the Sidney & Agnes Swennes residence is representative of the Tudor Revival style popular in the US during the 1920s and 1930s, reflected by its steeply pitched roof, prominent exterior chimney and gabled vestibules. This is the first home in Lafayette with a wide picture window facing west to capture the mountain views. Local contractors included Loyd & Phil Howe, William Brown, Lou Starkey, Floyd Williams, and William Yocum.
In the spring of 1945 Sidney Swennes (via Minnesota and Colorado Springs) purchased from Ralph Kemp the Highway Drug Store, then located at 101 Public Road. After a few years Swennes moved the business to 111 S. Public Road. He sold the business to Dewitt Sullivan in 1965, who sold the business to Dave & Vicki Trumbo in 1983.

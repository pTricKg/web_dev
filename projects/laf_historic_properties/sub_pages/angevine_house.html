Angevine House

Angevine House
610 E. Simpson Street

Built 1890
5BL.8860
Local Register 08/15/2000
 

The house was built in 1890 by S.J. Gildner from brick made by the Lafayette Kiln. Gildner was an agent for the Burlington & Missouri Railroad. Some time around 1902 he sold the house to Harry Noble, who was a master mechanic at the Simpson Mine, located just across Foote Street. Harry Noble was born in England in 1865 and arrived in Denver in 1887. As the family grew, Mr. Noble was prompted to build a two-room, one story, brick addition to the rear of the ho

use. He later added a second story to the new addition and eventually added a kitchen, bedroom and bath to the rear of the house. During summer months the Nobles would clear and grade the large flat expanse east of the house and neighbors from throughout the community would come and play croquet.
The house was sold to Merrill Angevine in 1937. Mr. Angevine was a member of City Council from 1967 to 1969, and again from 1976 to 1980.

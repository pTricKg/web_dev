Weiler House

Weiler House
401 E. Baseline Road

Built 1900
5BL.8858
Local Register 06/27/2000
 

The Weiler House was built in 1900, but it is unclear due to lack of accurate records who actually owned the house until 1923. Jacob Weiler purchased the house in 1923 and occupied it with his wife Lillie and their eight children. Jacob Weiler worked for the mines over the years as a pump man, fire boss and foreman. The house is somewhat larger than what we think of as a "miner's house" and is the cornerstone of the First Union Neighborhood. The young miners would often come by the house to pick up their work schedules, where they would socialize with Mr. Weiler's four teenage daughters.
In 1940, the house was furnished with indoor plumbing, installed in a room on the back porch. The one story structure is two rooms wide, three deep, including a living room, two bedrooms, dining room, kitchen and a sunroom on the east side, called the "sleeping porch" by the Weiler children. The Weiler House was placed on the Lafayette Register of Historic Properties in 2000.

Albert and Rosie James House

Albert and Rosie James House
310 W. Simpson Street

Built1957  
BL.8880
Local Register 11/18/2008
 

This residence is representative of dwellings built in Lafayette after WWII at the undeveloped edges of the city. It is an excellent example of the Ranch-type dwelling reflected by the hipped roof, modes front porch, overhanging eaves, and absence of traditional decorative details.
Built in 1957 as a wedding gift for Albert and Rosie James, this was the last house built by the Lafayette Construction, a local home building business owned by the James brothers. Lafayette Construction built 26 homes in Lafayette in the 1950s. Albert was a coal miner and WWII veteran and Rose worked at the University of Colorado and the pool hall on Public Road known as Rosie’s.

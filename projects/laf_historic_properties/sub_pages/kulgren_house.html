Kulgren House

Kulgren House
209 E. Cleveland Street

Built 1890
5BL.817
National Register 05/20/1983;
Local Register 09/21/1999
 

The Kulgren House was constructed in 1890. Andrew Kulgren owned a harness shop at 400 E. Simpson Street, where he made and repaired harnesses and shoes, and like many local merchants, he settled in one of the neighborhoods adjacent to Simpson Street. Mr. Kulgren served on the Town Council for three years. This seemed to have set a precedent for residents at this address, as the next owner, Ben Cundall, served on the Council for thirteen years, including four terms as mayor.
Mary Kulgren ran a boarding house in the home. The small building at the rear of the property was the wash house for the miners, who were expected to clean up there after a day in the mines. Like all good housewives in town, Mrs. Kulgren waged constant battle against the ubiquitous coal dust.

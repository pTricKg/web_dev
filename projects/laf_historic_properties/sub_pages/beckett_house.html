Beckett House

Beckett House
307 E. Cleveland Street

Built1896
5BL.7576
Local Register 04/07/2009
 

This vernacular building represents Lafayette’s coal mining era (1889-1946) and was home to a Lafayette postmaster, merchant and mine foreman. Its architectural features include shingled gable ends, bay windows, and decorative porch banisters. The house represents the late Victorian style in Lafayette. It’s most decorative feature is the attached porch gazebo, which alludes to the Queen Anne style.
This is the home of Edgar Beckett, longtime postmaster, and his wife Georgia. Edgar Beckett was appointed postmaster in 1897 by President McKinley. He was postmaster 13 years and weighmaster at the Simpson Mine.  Son Rexford Beckett grew up in the house and was part of the team at Great Western Sugar Company who invented one of the first mechanical sugar beet harvesters. Daughter Adelia was the first woman pharmacist in Ft. Collins in 1928. Sons Leland and Norman were volunteer firefighters who set a world record on the town hose team in 1924.

The Courthouse Towers

 

The red rocks of Arches National Park were deposited in layers over millions of years.
Later, erosion carved them into the features seen today. Water, ice, and very hot and cold temperatures cause erosion.

Seas covered this region 300 million years ago. When they dried up they left behind thousands
of feet of salt. Later, layers of sand and other sediments covered the salt. When buried, the
sediments were compressed into sedimentary rock. The buried salt moved under the pressure of the overlying sediment like toothpaste in a tube. As the salt moved, it ran into underground faults or barriers that caused the salt to go upward.

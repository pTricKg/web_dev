Partition Arch

 

The plants at Arches National Park would not be as healthy or numerous without the help of organisms like cyanobacteria. Cyanobacteria live on top of the soil. They have sticky sheaths,
which bind to individual grains of sand and absorb water. They are invisible to us when they are young. When they are at least 50 years old, fungi, algae and mosses grow with the cyanobacteria to form a crust. The crust is called Cryptobiotic Soil Crust.
